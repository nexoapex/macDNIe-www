AutoFirma on Mac: what it is and how macDNIe relates to it

Guide updated: 20 July 2026

AutoFirma is the Spanish government's official e-signature application, and many government sites invoke it directly to sign a submission. macDNIe doesn't force it out: it's interoperable with AutoFirma, and it separately handles browser sign-in and standalone document signing on its own, without leaving your Mac.

What is AutoFirma?

AutoFirma is the e-signature client distributed free of charge by the Government of Spain. Many Spanish e-government sites (Agencia Tributaria, Social Security, DGT and others) invoke it from the browser when a submission needs to be signed on the spot. macDNIe is not AutoFirma or an official substitute for it: it's an independent application by nexoapex that reads your DNIe, signs you in and signs documents on its own.

Does macDNIe replace AutoFirma?

No. Sites that invoke AutoFirma directly to sign a submission still need it — that doesn't change. What macDNIe offers is interoperability with AutoFirma — it doesn't interfere with it, and you can have both installed on the same Mac — plus it independently handles two things that don't always require AutoFirma: signing in to a browser session, and signing a document that isn't part of a government-site submission flow.

Do I need to install Java for any of this?

Not for macDNIe: it's a native macOS application, with no Java, no system configuration and no profiles. You only need macOS 13 or later (Intel or Apple Silicon), a USB contact card reader, your DNIe and your PIN.

What about Firefox?

Firefox doesn't use the native macOS integration (CryptoTokenKit) that Safari, Chrome and Edge rely on. That's why macDNIe ships its own PKCS#11 module, available in the direct-download edition, so your DNIe certificate shows up in Firefox too.

More on what macDNIe does or how to get it. If you're looking for help with a blocked PIN or an expired certificate, see the blocked or expired PIN guide.

Does macDNIe replace AutoFirma?

No. It's interoperable with AutoFirma, not a forced replacement. Sites that invoke AutoFirma directly to sign still need it; macDNIe doesn't interfere and both can coexist on the same Mac.

Do I need to install Java to use macDNIe?

No. macDNIe is a native macOS application: no Java, no system configuration, no profiles. Just a Mac on macOS 13+, a USB contact card reader, your DNIe and your PIN.

Can I sign in to the Tax Agency or Social Security without AutoFirma?

In Safari, Chrome and Edge, yes: macDNIe uses macOS's native integration (CryptoTokenKit) so your certificate shows up in the browser's own dialog. In Firefox, macDNIe adds its own PKCS#11 module in the direct-download edition.

Does macDNIe work in Firefox, where AutoFirma can be trickier?

Yes. Firefox doesn't use macOS's native integration, so macDNIe includes its own PKCS#11 module — in the direct-download edition — so you can sign in from Firefox like any other browser.

Unofficial product. macDNIe is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Dirección General de la Policía, the Ministry of the Interior or the Government of Spain. "DNIe" refers to the Spanish electronic national identity document, issued by the Dirección General de la Policía. All DNIe processing happens on your device; no personal data is ever transmitted.
